The Crooked Mile Is Here: What It Is and Why I Made It

Matthias Moore
April 28, 2026
3 min read

Every supplement I've ever run at my home table started as a single image in my head. For The Crooked Mile, it was a road through the woods that didn't end where it should. The party walks in. They walk for hours. The trees don't change. The light doesn't shift. And eventually they start to realize the road is taking them somewhere they didn't choose.

That image sat with me for months before I started writing. It wasn't until I had a concrete reason — a table that needed something dark, self-contained, and ready in three days — that it became a real adventure.

Why Edenfall?

I wanted to build a setting that rewarded drop-in play. Most campaign worlds ask Dungeon Masters to do a lot of reading before they can run confidently. Edenfall is designed the other way: you run the adventure, and the setting reveals itself through play. The Crooked Mile is the first entry point.

The name Edenfall came from a simple observation about fantasy settings: most of them are either in decline or on the verge of greatness. I wanted a world that felt mid-fall — past the peak, not yet at the bottom. A region where the institutions are crumbling, the roads are less safe than they used to be, and the wilderness has grown strange. That tension gives Dungeon Masters a lot to work with without requiring a full history lesson.

"A DM should be able to read a single adventure, run it cold, and have everything they need in front of them."

What's Next

The Edenfall Collection will grow one adventure at a time. Each volume is self-contained but leaves threads for a longer arc if the table wants to follow them. Volume 2 is already in development — it takes the party somewhere very different, but the roots of what's happening in The Crooked Mile will matter.

Patreon supporters will see Volume 2 first, including early playtesting drafts and behind-the-scenes design notes. If you want to follow along as the collection grows, that's the best place to do it.

The Crooked Mile is available now on DriveThruRPG as a pay-what-you-want PDF, and on Patreon for early access to everything that comes next.
